The NameNavnet

Who is Jagannath?Hvem er Jagannath?

The name Jagannath comes from two Sanskrit words: "Jagat" meaning universe, and "Natha" meaning Lord or master. He is thus the Lord of the entire universe — a form of Lord Krishna that is worshipped as the Supreme Absolute Truth in His most merciful and accessible aspect.

Lord Jagannath is most famous from the ancient temple city of Puri in Odisha, India, where He has been worshipped for thousands of years. His form is distinctive and unlike any other deity image — large round eyes, no arms or legs visible, a broad smile. This form is said to represent Krishna in the mood of hearing the pastimes of His own devotees — so absorbed in ecstasy that His limbs withdraw.Navnet Jagannath kommer fra to sanskritord: "Jagat" der betyder univers, og "Natha" der betyder Herre. Han er således hele universets Herre — en form af Herre Krishna, der tilbedes som den Højeste Absolutte Sandhed i Sin mest barmhjertige og tilgængelige aspekt.

Herre Jagannath er mest berømt fra den gamle tempelby Puri i Odisha, Indien, hvor Han er blevet tilbedt i tusinder af år. Hans form er karakteristisk og ulig nogen anden gudebillede — store runde øjne, ingen arme eller ben synlige, et bredt smil.

The Temple in CopenhagenTemplet i København

ISKCON DanmarkISKCON Danmark

The International Society for Krishna Consciousness (ISKCON), also known as the Hare Krishna movement, was founded in 1966 by His Divine Grace A.C. Bhaktivedanta Swami Prabhupada. The Copenhagen temple at Skjulhøj Allé 44 has served the Danish community and visitors for decades, offering daily worship, spiritual education, and prasadam — sanctified vegetarian food — freely to all.

The presiding deities of the temple are Sri Sri Radha-Madhavasundara along with Lord Jagannath, Baladeva, and Subhadra. The temple follows the Gaudiya Vaishnava tradition, which emphasises the chanting of the Hare Krishna mantra as the primary spiritual practice for this age.International Society for Krishna Consciousness (ISKCON), også kendt som Hare Krishna-bevægelsen, blev grundlagt i 1966 af Hans Guddommelige Nåde A.C. Bhaktivedanta Swami Prabhupada. Templet i København på Skjulhøj Allé 44 har tjent det danske samfund og besøgende i årtier og tilbyder daglig tilbedelse, åndelig uddannelse og prasadam — helliggjort vegetarisk mad — gratis til alle.
